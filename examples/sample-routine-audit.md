## Section-by-section comparison

### Wake & first hour

The 7:00 AM wake is fine in absolute terms — sleep duration of ~7.5 h hits the U-shaped optimum from `sleep-duration-and-quality.md` (Cappuccio et al., 2010). The problem is **schedule consistency**: shifting to 9 AM on weekends is a 2-hour swing. The sleep note flags irregular schedules as an independent CVD/metabolic risk factor even when average duration is adequate.

**Phone scrolling in bed** also drags out the wake transition without earning anything. The optimized workday has movement and light in the first 30 minutes — this routine has neither.

### Morning sunlight

**Missing entirely.** From `sunlight-exposure-and-vitamin-d.md`: 10–30 min of outdoor morning light within an hour of waking advances circadian phase and improves sleep onset by ~22 min. Driving to errands does not count — windshield glass blocks the relevant wavelengths. This is one of the cheapest, highest-leverage changes available and directly compounds with the sleep-consistency gap above.

### Breakfast / protein distribution

Skipping breakfast pushes the user to **2 protein feedings/day (lunch ~30 g, dinner ~50 g)**. From `protein-intake.md`, the evidence supports 3–5 feedings of ~0.4 g/kg each across the day for maximal muscle protein synthesis. Two large doses ≠ four moderate doses.

For a ~80 kg adult that's ~30–40 g per meal × 4 meals. Current intake is plausibly under target on lifting days when MPS demand is highest.

### Coffee

4 cups across 7 AM – 2 PM is **inside the 3–5 cup window** from `daily-coffee-consumption.md` and the cutoff before noon is mostly clean (one cup at 2 PM is the marginal case). The bigger issue is the oat milk — the coffee study's mortality findings come from black or minimally-additive coffee. Not a disaster, but worth noting.

### Sitting

**This is the largest gap.** From `prolonged-sitting.md`: the prescription is to break sitting every 30 minutes regardless of total exercise volume. The current routine is ~9 hours of near-continuous sitting (8 AM–6 PM with one bathroom break, plus 8:30–11 PM on the couch). Lifting 2x/week does not cancel this — the sitting study finds the every-30-min break is independently protective.

### Steps

5,500/day average. From `daily-walking.md`: the 47% all-cause mortality benefit (Ding et al., 2025) is anchored to ~7,000 steps/day with most gains between 5,000 and 7,000. The user is 1,500 steps short — about 15 min of additional walking. The 30-min dog walk is good (clears the "one continuous bout" criterion); the gap is incidental movement during work hours.

### Resistance training

2x/week × 45 min. The optimized workday calls for 3x/week. Below target but not by much — adding a Saturday session would close it.

### Creatine

Zero. From `daily-creatine-use.md`: 5 g/day is the standard dose, training-amplifies-the-benefit is the headline. Without lifting, the effect is modest; with the 2x/week lifting that's already happening, it stacks. Closing this gap is essentially free (~$0.30/day, water).

### Evening / sleep ramp-down

2.5 hours of screens before bed. From `sleep-duration-and-quality.md`: evening blue light suppresses melatonin and delays sleep onset. The user's actual sleep duration is okay, but quality is likely degraded.

### Alcohol

One glass of wine with dinner, daily. From `alcohol-consumption.md`: alcohol is an IARC Group 1 carcinogen with no safe threshold — even light intake measurably raises risk for at least seven cancers (oral cavity, pharynx, larynx, esophagus, colorectum, liver, female breast). The popular "moderate drinking is heart-healthy" J-curve has been ruled out by Mendelian randomization studies; the apparent benefit was confounding from healthier overall lifestyles among moderate drinkers, not a causal effect of alcohol.

Second-order effect: alcohol fragments REM and deep sleep architecture, compounding with the screen exposure noted above. The user's sleep duration looks fine on paper but is likely lower-quality than it appears.

## Ranked priorities — fix in this order

1. **Break sitting every 30 minutes during work hours.** Largest effect size, currently zero adherence. Set a recurring 30-min timer. Stand or walk 2–5 min when it fires. Source: `prolonged-sitting.md`.
2. **Add 10–15 minutes of morning outdoor light within an hour of waking.** Directly improves sleep onset (~22 min, per `sunlight-exposure-and-vitamin-d.md`) and the deficit is currently ~100% of target. Walk the dog in the morning instead of evening to combine this with steps.
3. **Stabilize weekend wake time to within 1 hour of weekdays.** Irregular schedule is an independent risk factor (`sleep-duration-and-quality.md`) and undoes much of the benefit of 7.5h average duration.
4. **Add a third protein feeding.** Easiest path: a 30 g shake or Greek yogurt mid-morning, which also makes the fasting window optional rather than load-bearing. Source: `protein-intake.md`.
5. **Drop the daily glass of wine.** No safe threshold for cancer risk per `alcohol-consumption.md`; cutting it also restores the deep-sleep quality the screen-time fix is trying to protect. The only item on this list defined by avoidance rather than action.
6. **Start 5 g creatine/day.** Free win that compounds with existing lifting. Source: `daily-creatine-use.md`.

Three other items (third lifting session, 1,500 more steps, oat milk in coffee) are real but smaller and partially fixed by the changes above — adding the morning walk closes most of the step gap on its own.
