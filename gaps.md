# Gaps

Topics that aren't yet in the vault but probably should be. Listed roughly in order of evidence strength and daily relevance for a sedentary working adult.

If you want to contribute, this is the shortlist. Read [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md) and the [format guide](./health-note-format-guide.md) before opening a PR.

## High-priority

These have strong evidence bases, broad daily relevance, and clean primary sources.

- **Hydration / water intake** — tied to cognitive performance, kidney function, and exercise capacity. The creatine note touches on water briefly but there's no standalone evidence review.
- **Stress / cortisol management** — chronic psychological stress is an independent CVD and mortality risk factor. The evidence on structured breathing (physiological sigh, box breathing), meditation, and social connection is now well-supported.
- **Resistance training programming** — referenced in both the creatine and sitting notes but has no dedicated file. Frequency, volume, progressive overload, compound vs. isolation movements.
- **Omega-3 / fish oil** — strong evidence for triglyceride reduction, depressive symptom reduction, and potentially CVD event reduction. ASCEND and VITAL trials are key anchors.

## Medium-priority

Strong evidence but narrower populations or smaller effect sizes.

- **Screen time / blue light** — evening blue light exposure suppressing melatonin and shifting circadian phase. Less robust than the others but directly relevant to a desk worker.
- **Social connection / loneliness** — Holt-Lunstad's meta-analyses show loneliness carries a mortality risk equivalent to ~15 cigarettes/day. Surprisingly powerful and often ignored.
- **Fiber / ultra-processed food** — converging evidence on cardiometabolic risk and all-cause mortality. The challenge is the breadth of what "ultra-processed" covers.
- **Cardio zones / aerobic base** — Zone 2 specifically is having a cultural moment but the underlying evidence is older and worth cleaning up.
- **Cold / heat exposure** — saunas have stronger evidence (Finnish cohorts) than cold plunges. Both popular, both widely overclaimed.

## Lower-priority or less evidence

Worth a note eventually but the evidence is thinner or the daily relevance lower.

- **Fasting windows / time-restricted eating** — evidence is mixed and the popular framing has run ahead of the data.
- **Breathwork** (separate from stress) — short-term physiological effects are real; long-term outcomes data is thinner.
- **Specific supplements** other than creatine — case-by-case. Vitamin D is already covered in the sunlight note.

## How to propose a new topic

Open an issue with the topic, why it belongs in the vault (one paragraph), and 1–3 candidate primary sources. If it's a fit, the PR follows the format in [`health-note-format-guide.md`](./health-note-format-guide.md).
